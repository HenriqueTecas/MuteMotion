Frequently Asked Questions

Get quick answers to common questions about MuteMotion.

How does MuteMotion actually work?

MuteMotion uses parametric speaker technology to create a focused beam of sound. Ultrasonic waves (above human hearing) carry the siren sound and demodulate in the air to create audible sound only in a specific direction. Our system includes cameras that detect vehicles and a mechanical system that precisely aims the sound beam where it's needed.

Is MuteMotion compatible with existing ambulances?

Yes, MuteMotion is designed as a retrofit solution that can be installed on existing ambulance fleets. Our system integrates with standard emergency vehicle electrical systems and can be mounted externally without major modifications to the vehicle.

What regulatory requirements does MuteMotion meet?

MuteMotion is being developed to comply with all relevant EU medical device and vehicle equipment regulations. We are working closely with regulatory experts to ensure our technology meets all necessary standards for emergency vehicle equipment. Our pilots with INEM will help validate compliance with operational requirements.

What is the expected return on investment for EMS providers?

EMS providers can expect ROI from multiple factors: improved patient outcomes leading to better quality metrics, reduced stress-related complications during transport, improved working conditions for EMS personnel, and potential reductions in noise-related complaints. How does weather affect the directional sound technology?

Our parametric speaker technology has been designed to function effectively across various weather conditions. While extremely heavy rain or snow can have minor effects on sound propagation, the system automatically adjusts power levels to maintain effectiveness. The targeting system works in all lighting conditions including night operations.

What maintenance is required?

MuteMotion requires minimal maintenance. The system includes self-diagnostic features that monitor performance. Regular check-ups are recommended during standard ambulance maintenance intervals. Our service contracts include software updates and technical support to ensure optimal operation throughout the system's lifecycle.
